# Working Group Management

Tracks how we engage the project working group — what we bring forward, when, how we frame it, and what we capture from each session.

**Why this exists:** The working group is a recurring forum that helps shape the workflow design — not just review finished artifacts. Treating each session as a casual conversation loses the input. Treating it as a formal review wastes the group's time on items that aren't ready. This file maintains the discipline in between.

---

## Principles for what gets brought to the working group

The working group's time is a finite resource. Use these criteria to decide what to bring:

**Bring forward:**
- Design questions where multiple reasonable answers exist and stakeholder input shapes the choice
- Cross-team implications that need representatives from multiple lenses in the same room
- Items that need group-level alignment before going to individual sign-offs (so individual reviewers aren't the first to surface objections)
- Beta team feedback that suggests changing previously-aligned design

**Don't bring forward:**
- Items where one stakeholder's input is sufficient (go to that stakeholder directly)
- Routine configuration mapping with no decision required
- Items where we haven't done the prep work to frame a real question
- Items already aligned that we just want to inform on (use a status update, not group time)

## How to frame an item for the working group

For each item being brought, prepare a short framing — typically 5-10 minutes of group time, sometimes 20-30 if the topic is complex. Structure:

1. **Context** (30 seconds) — what part of the workflow this is about
2. **The question** (1-2 sentences) — what specifically you need from the group
3. **Options** (3 max, with tradeoffs) — what the alternatives are and what each prioritizes
4. **Current recommendation** (if any) — what you'd do absent further input
5. **What you're not asking** — explicitly out of scope to keep the discussion focused

Save these framings as you prepare them — they're useful even if discussion shifts the framing, and they become input to `design-rationale.md`.
